Four Freedoms Foundation

Located on Roosevelt Island, the Four Freedoms Foundation museum utilizes an open ended interpretation of Roosevelts Four Freedoms Speech through a variety of spatial experiences scattered across a “field condition”. Each of the galleries can be utilized freely by curators, to convey their interpretations of the Four Freedoms philosophy set forth by Roosevelt related to freedom from fear, and want and freedom of speech and worship. This emphasizes a universal and ever-changing identity throughout time. A sense of freedom, fluid movement and visibility between inside and outside unifies the Four Freedoms Gallery's experience.

The Four Freedoms Foundation utilizes living materials native to the region in the design to provide food and habitat for native insects and birds. The garden terrace green wall features six different species, five of which are native to the area. English Ivy (Hedera helix) and Woodland stonecrop (Sedum ternatum) an evergreen vine and succulent evergreen provide yearlong green materiality. The remaining five species serve as colorful, changing materials with a variety of bloom periods throughout the year.

The screened light wells in the Four Freedoms Galleries are placed in the darkest areas of the plan to bring diffused light down to the ground plane. Each screened space is sized to accommodate small exhibitions and installations while still allowing visitor movement through the space and view across the “field”. Visitors can flow along curved exhibition surfaces and through and around exterior exhibitions and garden terraces.
